2022 Year in Review

‌

2022 was a great year at all levels, where we were able to continue serving women across Jordan and contributing to saving lives.

In this e-newsletter we are sharing with you a glimpse of 2022 achievements.

Thank you for being part of our journey!

The Jordan Breast Cancer Program Team

1. Service Delivery of Breast Cancer Screening and Early Detection

In 2022, Jordan Breast Cancer Program continued to develop and support infrastructure and quality upgrade for early detection of breast cancer services across all sectors to ensure better availability and accessibility to high quality early detection services across Jordan. This includes renovation, equipment donation, training of cadres, preparation for accreditation, and workflow enhancement support. Also, JBCP has worked on building the capacities of the healthcare providers (HCP) involved in the process of breast cancer screening and early detection, in addition to technical and quality control training for the radiographers in order to produce high quality images that will contribute to delivering improved breast cancer screening and early detection services. More details are mentioned below.

JBCP Mobile Mammography Units

JBCP has two Mobile Mammography Units (MMU) that are allocated at different places across Jordan in order to provide screening services to under-served and under-privileged women. Last year, 3,788 women were screened through the mobile units in three governorates; Zarqa, Balqa (Der Alla), and Irbid.

1.2 Improving Quality of Service Delivery

Breast Cancer Guidelines National Committee

As part of the Jordan Breast Cancer Program (JBCP) accountability towards developing and improving the quality of early detection services for early detection, and under the leadership of the King Hussein Cancer Center (KHCC), a National Committee of experts was formed to review and develop national guidelines for Breast Cancer Screening and Diagnosis. Several meetings were held, and the updated guidelines are expected to be issued by Feb 2023.

Success Story: Collaboration with Al-Bashir Breast Imaging Unit to Be an Integrated High-Quality Unit

The program has worked closely with Al-Basheer hospital on preparing the breast imaging unit as an integrated unit of high-quality services as it is considered the main referral hospital for the Ministry of Health. That included two female doctors undergoing a fellowship for a one year at the King Hussein Cancer Center, providing other necessary training for all working cadres, applying accreditation standards to ensure the quality-of-service collaboration with the HCAC, and providing the unit with the necessary devices and equipment to provide services appropriately.

2. Awareness and Social Behavioral Change Communication to Improve Demand on Breast Cancer Early Detection.

JBCP under this pillar focuses on building a strong and well-educated community that voice JBCP’s message of the importance of breast cancer screening and early detection. In order to alter behaviors, it is vital to have access to the community through several tactics and work on raising awareness and creating behavioral change through the Social Behavioral Change Communication (SBCC) model we adopt.
In order to channel JBCP’s message through the community, it is necessary to build advocates from within. Change induction is initiated by integrating the at the level of the community while so provision of the service in order to meet the increase in screening service demand.

"Innovative Integrated Social Behavior Change in Women's Health" Project, in Irbid, Zarqa and Aqaba governorates.

With a grant from MSD for mothers -a global initiative to reduce maternal mortality-, JBCP is implementing a three years’ project that provides comprehensive women health counselling and health promotion activities with special focus on maternal health; particularly topics of family planning, breast feeding, and breast cancer screening. Halfway through the project, trained community health workers were able to reach 32,000 women through conducting 15,444 (one-to-one) and 1,775 (one-to-group) sessions. Utilizing the newly developed skills of 72 trained HCPS, 6,108 women (Jordanians & Refugees) were provided with health services in designated health centers (and through JBCP mobile Unit).

Kick-off of the Blended Learning & Behavior Change for Local Development Project

JBCP has launched supported by the Spanish Agency for International Development Cooperation (AECID), launched the Blended Learning & Behavior change for Local Development Project which aims mobilize the community through one-to-one, one-to-group and one-to-mass awareness activities and create a community that supports breast cancer early detection and cancer prevention. The project also generates groundbreaking research and evidence on women’s access to NCDs care and health equity challenges in Jordan. The project comprehensively targets the community, including Jordanians and Syrian refugees.

Concluding year one of implementation, JBCP reached 24,767 women through the program activities conducted by more than 100 trained Community Health Workers raising community awareness, changing negative social behaviors and advocating for better breast cancer screening, early detection, and cancer prevention practices.

JBCP has trained 45 champions where they commenced implementation of innovative initiatives to voice out JBCP messages to their peers in their community. Overall, 277 members of the community were reached through 14 initiatives executed by the champions in the targeted governorates.

October Breast Cancer Awareness Campaign

Every October, the international month for breast cancer awareness we start seeing the pink color spreading everywhere as an effort from all entities working in the field of cancer control to shed more light on Breast cancer.

In cooperation with 12 Arab countries (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Lebanon, Egypt, Algeria, Palestine, Sudan, Bahrain, Morocco, Libya, Oman and Kuwait); JBCP has launched the 7th regional campaign: An Image for Life. Get Tested- صورة للحياة افحصي. In this campaign Jordan Breast Cancer Program has emphasized the message to all women aged 40 and above to get screened through mammography.

The campaign included different activities that targeted all age groups at all governorates. It included many educational and interactive lectures in schools and universities, preparing multiple informative sessions in private, public sectors, and community-based organizations, distributing educational material across a Jordan and the media campaign through visual media, radio broadcast and social media platforms. It also included illuminating in pink many historical and cultural sites in Jordan. During the campaign JBCP collaborated with the different healthcare providers to provide free clinical breast examinations, and subsidized rates for mammogram images.

October Campaign Achievements:

  • 9,408 Mammography images were conducted and 42,000 women got CBE throughout Jordan
  • 40,000 People were reached through 40 Roadshows implemented in malls and medical centers
  • 5 million People were reached through lectures, roadshows, home visits, media and social media exposure.
  • 855 Locations branded with the campaign theme.
  • 900,000 Educational & Promotional materials were delivered to educate on the importance of breast cancer screening
  • More than 2,000,000 views on TV campaign Ad
  • Two million SMS has been sent to reach landing page of October campaign.

October Think Pink Activities:

Every October, JBCP launches the “Think Pink” initiative which is an initiative to encourage the entire community to raise awareness on breast cancer early detection and support the cause, positioning breast cancer as a community and public health priority. Last October, more than 2,500 Think Pink” events have been conducted in collaboration with private and public sectors and NGO’s Including but not limited to, hospitals, medical centers, corporates, associations, universities, schools, fitness & Wellness Centers, Ministries and embassies, reaching more than 60,000 community members with our message.
